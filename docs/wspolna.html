— 01 · The development

Two homes,
with mirrored layouts.

Wspólna is two homes built from a single mirrored plan. From the street, units share a path and a parking area, but that's where the overlap ends. One home faces east, the other west, each with its own garden, its own light, its own side to itself.

The setting. Łomianki is a town in a north-west suburb of Warsaw nestled between Kampinos National Park & Vistula River. Wspólna is a recently renovated, residential street offering a peacful setting while being located a short distance from town's robust services.

The home. Three storeys, flat-roof modernism, with a balcony and a terrace at the back. North-east-facing back gardens, full-height glazing, three bedrooms and a large utility & storage room above. White exterior façade, anthracite windows, doors, balcony rails and roof flashing. Two timeless colors that don't fade, don't date, and don't need repainting to stay looking right.

The two units. Units 1 and 2 are mirror images of one another, built from a single plan. From the street, they share an entrance path and parking, beyond that, everything turns private: opposite orientations, separate gardens, each home with its own light and its own side of the plot.

A short drive to the centre.

Reach Warsaw within 30 minutes by car. Local buses connect to the Młociny metro station.

Close to nature.

Quick 5 minute bicycle ride takes you to the scenic riverside of Vistula or Lake Kiełpińskie, alternatively reach within 3.5 km largest National Park in Poland - Kampinoski Park Narodowy.

A town, not a dormitory.

Łomianki stands on its own: kindergartens and schools, the ICDS sport and culture centre, health clinics, supermarkets and a weekend market — daily life handled without driving into the city.
