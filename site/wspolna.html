— 01 · The development

Two homes,
two distinct layouts.

Wspólna is two homes — Unit 1 and Unit 2 — sharing one plot and one architectural language, yet each drawn to its own floor plan. Not mirrored, not repeated: two individual houses behind a single, coherent façade.

The setting. Łomianki Dolne sits on the quiet, river side of Łomianki — a north-west suburb of Warsaw. Wspólna is a residential street a short walk from local shops and the school, with the Vistula and its riverside paths close by.

The home. Two storeys, flat-roof modernism, cantilevered eaves and a covered carport per unit. South-facing back gardens, full-height living-room glazing, three bedrooms and a large utility & storage room above. Materials are deliberately ordinary — white through-coloured render, anthracite aluminium, oak floors inside — so the building ages gracefully and runs cheaply.

The two units. Unit 1 and Unit 2 are not mirror images of one another. Each has its own floor plan, its own room proportions and its own relationship to the garden and the street — two genuinely different houses that happen to share a wall and a roofline.

— 05 · Specification

Honest materials.
Quietly over-built.

We don’t skimp where it doesn’t show. The boring specifications — the ones a buyer rarely reads — are where R2 spends most of its budget.

— 01 · Walls

Silka 24 · 20 cm wool.

Twenty-four-centimetre Silka calcium-silicate walls with 20 cm of mineral wool. Hush.

— 02 · Glazing

Triple, warm-edge.

Heat pump · UF.
