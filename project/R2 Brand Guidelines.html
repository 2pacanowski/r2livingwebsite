— Scene · Interior · Evening

Don’t — cliché developer photography

  • The keys-in-the-air pose, the handshake with the agent.
  • Couple clinking champagne in a staged white kitchen.
  • Drone shots of a manicured subdivision at golden hour.
  • Family of four, perfectly dressed, smiling straight to camera, arms around each other in front of the house.
  • Stock-photo models. Teal-and-orange grading. Lens flare. Empty staged rooms with single white tulips.
  • “Lifestyle” shots that have never met an actual life.

Do — documentary, ordinary, true

  • Backs of heads. Hands. Mid-laugh, mid-sentence, mid-pour.
  • One parent fully in frame, the other a shoulder at the edge.
  • Kids being kids: crumbs on the counter, socks, a half-drawn picture.
  • The unrenovated, the half-unpacked, the bare room with potential.
  • Polish autumn light — birch, oak, brick paths, a real Mazowsze sky.
  • Frames that could plausibly be from the family’s own phone — only better.

— Direction

Light
Natural only — window, garden, lamp. Never on-camera flash.
Lens
35 mm and 50 mm primes. Wide enough for context, never group-portrait wide.
People
Parents 30 – 40. Real residents wherever possible. Cast for warmth and ease, not symmetry.
Wardrobe
Their own clothes. Tees, jeans, knits, socks. Nothing borrowed for the shoot.
Palette
Muted, warm, true skin tones. No teal-orange grade.
Post
Subtle warm cast, a touch of grain. Crops are generous — never zoomed-in heads.
Where
R2 homes, Warsaw suburbs, Mazowieckie countryside. Place must read.
