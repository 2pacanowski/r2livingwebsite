One way of seeing.

Each prompt below is written for Nano Banana — Google’s Gemini 2.5 Flash Image — which responds best to descriptive natural language, not Midjourney-style flags. Paste, generate, iterate. Bring the keepers back and we’ll wire them into the brand sheet in place of the gradient stand-ins.

The universal style suffix.

Every R2 frame inherits the same look. To keep generations consistent across the eight scenes (and any future ones), append this block to the end of any prompt. Or paste it once and use Nano Banana’s edit mode to apply it across a set.

R2 Living — house style ·
Documentary lifestyle photography, not advertising. Polish family in modern suburban Warsaw architecture (clean modern duplex, brick path, oak, birch, no manicured perfection). Natural light only — window, garden, or single warm lamp — never on-camera flash, never golden-hour cinematic glow, never teal-orange color grading. True skin tones, muted warm palette: cream, oak, charcoal, deep moss green, soft stone. Real wardrobe — knits, tees, jeans, socks, never styled. Real bodies, real ages, real expressions — mid-laugh, mid-sentence, mid-pour, never camera-aware smiles. Generous framing on a 35mm or 50mm prime, eye-level or slightly low, shallow depth of field, mild film grain. Avoid: keys-in-the-air pose, handshake with agent, champagne clink, drone subdivision, single-white-tulip staging, group portrait centred to camera. The frame should plausibly come from the family’s own phone — only better lit and better composed. Photorealistic, 8K, subtle warm cast in post.

001 · Tuesday morning, half-eaten cereal.

A candid documentary photograph of a Polish mother, 32, with shoulder-length dark blonde hair tied in a loose low ponytail, wearing a soft cream cotton t-shirt and dark grey sweatpants. She is leaning with one elbow on the kitchen counter of a modern duplex home while her four-year-old daughter, also in pyjamas, picks raisins out of a half-eaten bowl of muesli on the counter. Late-morning daylight comes from a large window on the left — soft, warm, not cinematic. The kitchen is real and lived-in: oak veneer cabinetry, a stainless kettle, a single small succulent on the windowsill, a child’s drawing held to the fridge with a magnet. The mother is mid-yawn-into-a-smile, eyes on her daughter, not the camera. Shot on a 35mm lens, eye level, shallow depth of field. Aspect ratio 3:4 portrait. Then apply the R2 Living house style.

002 · The wall, before the wall.

An overhead three-quarter documentary photograph of a printed architectural floor plan unrolled on a wooden kitchen table. A woman’s hand, early thirties, no rings, slim silver bracelet on the wrist, holds a thin black marker mid-stroke, drawing a soft circle around an interior wall on the plan. A pale ceramic mug sits at the edge of the plan, leaving a faint coffee ring on the paper. To the side, partially in frame, a child’s wax-crayon drawing of a stick-figure family. Soft natural daylight from the right, no harsh shadows. Shallow depth of field with the marker tip in sharp focus, the rest gently soft. Warm muted palette — cream paper, blue-black ink, soft ceramic, brown wax crayon. The image captures the moment of decision, not the result. Aspect ratio 4:3 landscape. A documentary photograph of a Polish couple in their late thirties standing on the wooden back terrace of a modern duplex home, looking out at a bare autumn garden. The woman wears a charcoal wool sweater pushed up to her elbows, hair tied back, holding a ceramic mug in both hands. The man wears a deep-blue flannel shirt and dark jeans, hands in his pockets. The couple are mid-conversation, the woman gesturing softly toward a spot in the empty garden. Overcast Polish autumn light, soft and even. Shot from behind them at a three-quarter angle on a 50mm lens, shallow depth of field. Warm-muted palette dominated by moss greens, bronzes, soft greys — no blue sky, no golden hour. A still-life documentary photograph of an empty, recently finished room in a modern Polish family home — bare oak floor, soft white walls, a single tall window with no blinds. — Working with Nano Banana
  1. Paste a prompt. Generate three to four variants.
  2. Pick the closest. Use “edit” to nudge — never re-roll from scratch. Nano Banana is exceptional at consistent iteration.
  3. Maintain face / location continuity by referencing earlier outputs explicitly: “same mother as previous image, different room.”
  4. If a scene drifts cinematic, paste this fix: “Reduce contrast, neutralise highlights, mild film grain, no orange-and-teal grade, true Polish daylight.”
