— Round 03 · Inline serif lockup

Six ways to tune R² Living.

Same family — one typeface, one line, one breath. Each variant tweaks a single lever: italic vs roman, separator vs none, superscript proportion, spacing, and small-caps. Compare the large primary, dark, and moss versions for each.

— Option D1

Italic descriptor

Upright R² · Italic Living
The original
D1 · 01 R2Living
D1 · 02 R2Living
D1 · 03 R2Living

Reads as: calm, editorial. The italic gives “Living” warmth without breaking the line. Trade-off: two voices in one mark (roman + italic) — slightly more to manage in small sizes.

— Option D2

All roman

Upright R² · Upright Living
Quietest
D2 · 01 R2Living
D2 · 02 R2Living
D2 · 03 R2Living

Reads as: architectural and even-tempered — closer to a press masthead. Trade-off: loses the friendliness the italic brought; “Living” has to do all the warmth on its own.

— Option D3

Mid-dot separator

R² · Living
Punctuated
D3 · 01 R2·Living
D3 · 02 R2·Living
D3 · 03 R2·Living

Reads as: directory-style, technical, confident — “R² · Living” feels like a coordinate. Trade-off: the dot is precious if not well-kerned; harder to scale below 18 px.

— Option D4

Tucked superscript

Smaller, higher “²”
Most refined
D4 · 01 R2Living
D4 · 02 R2Living
D4 · 03 R2Living

Reads as: a real mathematical exponent — the “²” recedes, “R Living” reads first. Most precise of the set. Trade-off: the “²” gets fragile below 16 px; needs a tweaked “mark only” version for favicons.

— Option D5

All italic

Italic R² · Italic Living
Warmest
D5 · 01 R2Living
D5 · 02 R2Living
D5 · 03 R2Living

Reads as: the most emotional, hand-set, gallery-catalogue feel. Leans away from “tech” and toward “atelier.” Trade-off: at small sizes italics can feel a touch unstable; less authoritative on signage.

— Option D6

Letterspaced caps

Upright R² · L I V I N G
Most architectural
D6 · 01 R2Living
D6 · 02 R2Living
D6 · 03 R2Living

Reads as: chiselled, architectural, premium-real-estate — closest to a building plate. Trade-off: loses the “Living” warmth; tracking has to be tuned per size.

— Premium / atelier

D1, D4, D5 all sit here. D4 is the most precise of the three; D1 is the most balanced; D5 is the warmest.

— Editorial / architectural

D2 and D6 push toward press / masthead / building plate. D6 is the most monumental — works exceptionally well on hoarding.

— My pick

D4 · tucked superscript — the refinement of the smaller “²” fixes the readability issue you flagged and feels the most premium. We could pair it with D6 as the signage-only variant.
